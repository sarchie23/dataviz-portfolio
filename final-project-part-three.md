# Changes made since Part II
I made a lot of changes. I switched from Shorthand to a python-generated html file. If I could have used images of players, I think Shorthand would have worked well but I think the simplicity of a white background makes the interactive visualization pop and focuses your attention on those rather than other fancy features.  I tried to make it so you can see the charts and the text at the same time for ease of reading. It also mirrors the data journalism pieces that I was modeling my story after, like [Neil Paine's work](https://neilpaine.substack.com/). 

I changed the jersey figure and all my other charts to an interactive python figure using plotly. I updated my datasets to include all NCAA players who started playing after 2015, which allowed the trends to be more comprehensive than a small subset of elite players. I came up with a narrative that I wanted to tell. I decided to make this a series of articles on the transfer portal in women's college basketball because there is a lot of analysis that I did (and still want to do) that I did not include because I thought it might be better in a separate story that focused just on those sub-topics. I added the elite 8 analysis that I showed in my presentation on Monday. I made a timeline with the transfer portal inforamtion.
